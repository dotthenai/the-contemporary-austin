EXHIBITIONS

STRANGE PILGRIMS

SEPTEMBER 27, 2015 – JANUARY 24, 2016

Strange Pilgrims is the museum’s first large-scale, multi-site group exhibition for Fall 2015. Featuring fourteen international, cross-generational artists across three sites (the Jones Center, Laguna Gloria, and the Visual Arts Center in the Department of Art and Art History at The University of Texas at Austin), Strange Pilgrims is an open-ended, experiential journey through time, space, imagination, perception, and the senses.
We hope you’ll travel with us!

Artists

Charles Atlas, Trisha Baga, Millie Chen, Phil Collins, Andy Coolquitt, Ayşe Erkmen, Roger Hiorns, Nancy Holt, Lakes Were Rivers, Angelbert Metoyer, Bruce Nauman, Yoko Ono, Paul Sharits, Sofía Táboas

On View at the Jones Center, Laguna Gloria, and the Visual Arts Center in the Department of Art and Art History at The University of Texas at Austin

ARTISTS

current & upcoming

do ho suh

A South Korean artist, born 1962 in Seoul. He splits his time between New York City, London, and Seoul. He received a BFA in painting from the Rhode Island School of Design and an MFA in sculpture from Yale University.

john grade

An American artist, born 1970 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. He lives and works in Seattle. Grade creates large-scale sculptures that are exhibited internationally in museums, galleries, and outdoors in nature.

liam gillick

A British artist, born 1964 in Aylesbury, UK. He currently lives and works in New York. He is perhaps best known for a traveling retrospective titled Three Perspectives and a Short Scenario, shown at the Kunsthalle in Zurich.

MARIANNE VITALE

An American artist, born 1973 in East Rockaway, New York. She currently lives and works in New York. Her work has been seen in group exhibitions including the 2010 Whitney Biennial and in solo exhibitions including the Sculpture Center in New York (2009).

GREEN SCREEN FILM SERIES: WARHOL’S SLEEP

schedule 6:30-11:59p, Oct 30, 2015 placeLaguna Gloria

Reservations recommended; please check back for details.

Sleepwalk with Andy Warhol during a screening of the artist’s dreamy durational film Sleep, consisting of five hours and twenty-one minutes of Warhol’s friend John Giorno sleeping, projected in 16 mm (1963, 321 min., silent, black and white).

Programmed by Scott Stark for Experimental Response Cinema. Green Screen Film Series: Warhol’s Sleep is presented in collaboration with Experimental Response Cinema and in partnership with the Visual Arts Center.

LA DOLCE VITA

schedule 7—10p, Oct 15, 2015 placeLaguna Gloria

Held on the beautiful grounds of Laguna Gloria, La Dolce Vita Food and Wine Festival for Art Education showcases the culinary achievements of the best chefs and winemakers in Central Texas. La Dolce Vita is one of the city’s most anticipated fall events, with all proceeds benefiting The Contemporary Austin’s innovative art education programs that serve thousands of schoolchildren, families, and adults each year.

For questions about La Dolce Vita, please contact Blythe Bailey at bbailey@thecontemporaryaustin.org or 512 453 5312 x 255.
